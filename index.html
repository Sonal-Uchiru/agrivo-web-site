Enhancing The Livelihoods Of Small-Scale Paddy farmers In Sri Lanka Through Computational Approcahes For Inclusive and Equitable Agriculture

Get Started

Project Scope

Overall Project Description

Paddy cultivation, synonymous with rice farming, constitutes a vital cornerstone of Sri Lanka's food security and economic prosperity. Despite its significance, this practice confronts a slew of formidable challenges, encompassing inadequate furrow irrigation techniques, suboptimal fertilizer subsidy distribution, the absence of precise yield prediction models, and constrained access to invaluable research insights. In a concerted effort to surmount these multifaceted concerns, this research advances an innovative integrated system characterized by four distinct yet interconnected components. The inaugural component introduces a sophisticated application engineered for furrow irrigation planning, underpinned by the formidable capabilities of support vector machine and random forest algorithms. This novel irrigation planning application embarks on a mission to revolutionize rice cultivation by optimizing irrigation paths, elevating the efficiency of water usage, and mitigating resource wastage. The second component unveils a visionary priority-based fertilizer subsidy system, driven by the powerful Gradient Boosting technique. This revolutionary system stands poised to allocate agricultural subsidies with unprecedented efficiency, tailored precisely to the unique needs and circumstances of individual farmers. By leveraging data-driven insights, it promises to transcend the existing inefficiencies plaguing subsidy distribution, thereby empowering farmers and fostering equitable agricultural progress. Turning our gaze to the third component, the focus shifts towards democratizing the accessibility of research findings. Through the judicious application of text summarization techniques, this component seeks to distill intricate research findings into concise, comprehensible summaries. In doing so, it empowers small-scale farmers with the knowledge and insights required to make informed decisions, transcending language and literacy barriers. In the fourth and final component, a formidable yield prediction model takes center stage, underpinned by the robust Random Forest algorithm. This cutting-edge model, meticulously calibrated to accommodate climatic and soil variables, assumes the pivotal role of forecasting expected yields. It not only empowers farmers with the foresight required to optimize cultivation practices but also augments resource management, ultimately contributing to sustainable rice production and economic growth. The integrated system forged by these four components represents a holistic and visionary approach to enhancing the efficiency, productivity, and profitability of paddy farming in Sri Lanka.

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Furrow Irrigation

In Sri Lanka's dry zone rice cultivation, the absence of rain-fed fields leads to heavy reliance on natural water sources. Existing studies focus on improving irrigation systems, but there's a gap in precise irrigation path identification within individual paddy fields, which results in inefficient water use. A need exists for a more efficient irrigation pattern to conserve water and address specific challenges in paddy farming.

Fertilizer Subsidy

Numerous research studies investigate Sri Lanka's Fertilizer Subsidy Program, providing insights into its workings and suggesting policy changes. However, these studies do not offer concrete guidance for implementation under Sri Lanka's specific conditions, particularly concerning the timely fertilizer application. This gap in research highlights the necessity for tailored strategies to address the country's present challenges.

Research Dissemination

Small-scale farmers in Sri Lanka face challenges accessing and comprehending research findings. Barriers include limited communication channels between research institutes and farmers, the technical complexity of research papers, and reliance on informal networks for information. There is a need for improved communication strategies and simplified presentation of research findings to ensure small-scale farmers can access and apply the latest agricultural knowledge effectively.

Yield Prediction

Paddy yield prediction is essential for food security and environmental sustainability in Sri Lanka. Research studies have explored the impact of climate variables on yield predictions, but non-climatic factors like soil fertility and market demand are often overlooked. There is a need for more accurate prediction models that consider both climatic and non-climatic variables, with a focus on improving accessibility to rural farmers.

Research Problems

How to determine efficient furrow irrigation paths in a paddy field

How to allocate fertilizer subsidy to farmers with the highest need for fertilizer

How to distribute research findings to small-scale paddy farmers effectively

How to determine the expected yield of a given area accurately

Proposed Solution

Agrivo, our comprehensive agricultural solution, combines multiple technologies to address the diverse needs of small-scale paddy farmers in Sri Lanka. The Furrow Irrigation Component leverages GeoJSON, GeoLib, and Mapbox to streamline furrow irrigation management, ensuring efficient water distribution. In the Fertilizer Priority Distribution Component, Machine Learning and supervised learning classifier models are applied to create a priority-based system for timely fertilizer subsidies. The Research Dissemination Component utilizes Spacy, NLP (Natural Language Processing), NLTK (Natural Language Toolkit), and the BART model to extract, summarize, and deliver research findings effectively. The Yield Prediction Component employs machine learning techniques, including supervised learning regressors, to provide farmers with accurate crop yield predictions based on climate and soil data, enabling them to optimize their agricultural practices. With these integrated components, Agrivo empowers small-scale farmers with the tools and knowledge they need to enhance their productivity and promote sustainable agriculture.

Methodology

The overall system aims to address pressing challenges in paddy cultivation in Sri Lanka through a holistic approach consisting of four interconnected research components. Each component is meticulously designed to tackle specific issues faced by small-scale paddy farmers and contribute to the sustainable development of the agriculture sector in the region.

1. Mobile Application for Efficient Furrow Irrigation Planning

The first research component is centered around the development of a mobile application tailored to the needs of paddy farmers. This innovative tool empowers farmers to plan furrow irrigation efficiently. Water management is a critical concern in paddy cultivation, and this component addresses it by providing a user-friendly interface that allows farmers to schedule and optimize their irrigation practices. By enhancing water utilization and minimizing wastage, this component not only conserves a precious resource but also promotes sustainable farming practices.

2. Priority-Based Distribution in Fertilizer Subsidy Program

The second research component introduces a data-driven approach to enhance the effectiveness of the fertilizer subsidy program. By incorporating priority- based distribution mechanisms, this component ensures that farmers with the greatest need receive timely access to subsidized fertilizers. This not only optimizes the allocation of resources but also helps small-scale farmers improve the quality of their crops, ultimately leading to higher yields and economic benefits.

3. Research Dissemination for Informed Decision-Making

The third research component focuses on improving the dissemination of agricultural research findings to small-scale farmers. Informed decision- making is a cornerstone of successful farming, and this component strives to bridge the gap between complex research and accessible knowledge. Through the use of advanced Natural Language Processing (NLP) models, translations, and a user-friendly mobile application, small-scale farmers gain access to the latest agricultural insights, fostering better farming practices and increased agricultural productivity.

4. Mobile-Based Yield Prediction Considering Environmental Factors

The fourth research component utilizes mobile technology to predict farmers' expected yield. By integrating climatic and non-climatic variables, this component offers a powerful tool for farmers to anticipate and plan for crop yields accurately. Climate variability is a significant challenge in agriculture, and this component equips farmers with valuable information to adapt and mitigate risks effectively.

Collectively, these four research components synergize to enhance the efficiency, productivity, and profitability of paddy farming in Sri Lanka. By addressing critical aspects of irrigation, fertilizer distribution, research dissemination, and yield prediction, the system not only empowers farmers but also contributes to the long-term sustainability of agriculture in the region. Through technology-driven solutions and data-informed decision-making, the research project seeks to create a positive impact on the livelihoods of small- scale paddy farmers and the agricultural landscape of Sri Lank.
